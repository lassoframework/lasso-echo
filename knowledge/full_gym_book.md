## The frameworks (citable, from the book)
- Three Levers of Growth: churn (do people stay), sales (do you convert the people who show up), leads (do enough people enter the pipeline). Fix churn, fix sales, then increase leads. Paid ads are the last lever, not the first.
- Healthy metrics from the book: churn 3 to 6 percent; organic close rate 70 to 80 percent; industry average 4 to 5 new members per month; industry average roughly 30 leads per month.
- The Paid Ads Advantage (book figures): LASSO clients average roughly 77 leads per month vs industry about 30; 12 to 15 sign ups per month vs industry 4 to 5; roughly 45,000 dollars per month average client revenue vs industry about 18,750.
- The Testing Rule: member value times 3 equals minimum testing budget, per avatar.
- The 14 Day Rule: judge campaigns on 14 day windows, 30 during holidays. Never react to 7 day noise.
- The Halo Effect: four pillars are awareness, trust, frequency, demand. Most conversions never show in the ad dashboard. The only attribution that matters: did sign ups increase after ads started.
- 30 40 30 budget split (from the Shayla case study): 30 percent awareness, 40 percent traffic, 30 percent conversions.
- Client avatar law: if you are speaking to everyone, you are speaking to no one. You are the guide, not the hero. Clarity beats creativity. If you confuse, you lose.
- Copy law: people do not buy because your gym is cool, they buy because you solve a problem they cannot solve alone. Short copy captures attention, long copy captures commitment.
- Lead quality law: a true low quality lead is fake contact info, not a human who went quiet. Turn off autofill, turn on SMS verification (roughly 25 percent quality lift per Meta data cited in the book). A more expensive lead that shows up is cheaper than a cheap lead that ghosts you.
- Speed law: first touch within five minutes. The window of peak interest closes fast. Move engaged leads from text to a phone call.
- Sales law (Sherman): sales is diagnosis and guidance, not pressure. You must make a prescription. Sell from the top down. The first person to speak loses. Help first and do no harm.
- Follow up law: it takes 7 to 10 exposures before someone decides to do business with you. A no today does not mean no forever.
- Three pillars every client needs: fitness, nutrition, accountability.
